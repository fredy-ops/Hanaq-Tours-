Prepare for Your Andean Adventure

Essential advice on altitude, weather, and packing to ensure a safe and memorable journey.

Altitude Sickness: Prevention and Tips

Many of our tours take place at high elevations (Cusco is 3,400m). **Acclimatization is key** to enjoying your trip!

  • **Ascend Gradually:** Spend 1-2 days relaxing in Cusco before hiking.
  • **Stay Hydrated:** Drink plenty of water and avoid alcohol for the first 48 hours.
  • **Coca Tea:** The local remedy, mate de coca, can help alleviate symptoms.
  • **Rest:** Listen to your body and avoid strenuous activity immediately upon arrival.

Peruvian Seasons: Dry vs. Wet

Peru has two main seasons in the highlands:

Dry Season (April - October): Sunny days, cold nights. Best time for trekking (Inca Trail).
Wet Season (November - March): Warmer days, frequent rain showers. Less crowded and greener landscapes.

Visa and Health Information

Most travelers from the US, Canada, and Western Europe do not need a visa for stays up to 183 days. Always check with your local Peruvian embassy.

No vaccines are mandatory, but check with your doctor regarding Hepatitis A and Typhoid, especially if visiting the Amazon.
